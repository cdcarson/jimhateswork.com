Why do these cost so much you ask? "It's crazy!" you say. Well, crazy like a fox say I. Here's why it costs so much:

Step 1

First, I have to draw the damn thing and then trace the pattern onto the belt. This one is hand-drawn, entirely original, and it took three hours to do this.

 

Step 2

Then I hand carve the design into the belt with that little knife. This one took over two hours to carve and I'm sure I have carpel tunnel syndrome now, but I didn't charge the customer for that.

 

Step 3

Then I use the stamping tools to create depth and shading effects. This one took over four hours.

 

Finished Belt

Finally, I finish the belt with a three-step oiling, staining, and sealing process that creates the finished look above. In the end, you get a piece of art that should last longer than you, and it holds your pants up too. So, that's why it costs so much.
